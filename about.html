About Poultry Care

Poultry farming plays a vital role in food security and the economy across the globe. However, poultry diseases pose significant threats to bird health, farmer livelihoods, and food production. Early detection and intervention are essential to reduce mortality and economic losses.

Why Poultry Disease Detection Matters

Common Poultry Diseases Detected

  • Avian Influenza (Bird Flu): Highly contagious viral disease. Symptoms: respiratory distress, swollen head, blue comb/wattles. Requires immediate isolation and vet intervention.
  • Newcastle Disease: Affects respiratory, nervous, and digestive systems. Symptoms: coughing, tremors, green diarrhea. Vaccination is key for prevention.
  • Fowl Pox: Viral infection causing skin lesions and diphtheritic membranes. Spread by mosquitoes or contact. Treat with iodine and supportive care.
  • Coccidiosis: Protozoan intestinal disease. Symptoms include bloody droppings, lethargy, and weight loss. Controlled by anticoccidials and hygiene.
  • Infectious Bronchitis: Coronavirus affecting respiratory tract. Symptoms: coughing, sneezing, reduced egg production. Spread via air and contact.
